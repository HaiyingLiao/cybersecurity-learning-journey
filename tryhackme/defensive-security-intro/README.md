## Concepts I Learned

### 1. What Defensive Security Is

Defensive security focuses on **protecting** an organisation‚Äôs systems, networks, and data from attacks.  
It is the counterpart to offensive security, and its goal is to **prevent**, **detect**, and **respond** to threats.

### 2. The Cost of Poor Security

Weak or incomplete security controls can:

- Expose sensitive data
- Disrupt business operations
- Lead to severe financial losses
- Damage an organisation‚Äôs reputation

Strong defensive security is essential for business continuity.

---

## Key Areas of Defensive Security

### 1. **Monitoring & Detecting**

Watching for unusual or malicious activity using tools like SIEM, IDS, and logs.

### 2. **Incident Response**

Taking quick, structured action when a threat is detected to contain damage.

### 3. **Threat Intelligence**

Understanding attacker behaviour, tactics, and patterns to predict and defend against threats.

### 4. **Vulnerability Management**

Identifying and fixing weaknesses before attackers exploit them.

### 5. **Investigation & Analysis**

Reviewing alerts, logs, and evidence to determine what happened and how to respond.

These areas make up the core responsibilities of a blue-team analyst.

# Defence in Depth

Organisations do not rely on one tool or control. They build **layers of defence** so that if one fails, others still protect the organisation.

Examples:

- **Employee Training** (reduces human error)
- **Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)**
- **Firewalls**
- **Security Policies**

This layered approach ensures that even if an employee clicks a malicious link, other controls help prevent damage.

---

# Exploring the Security Operations Centre (SOC)

A **SOC** is the central hub for defensive security.  
It operates 24/7 and employs analysts who:

- Review security alerts
- Investigate anomalies
- Escalate or respond to incidents
- Monitor systems, networks, and logs

SOC analysts are the **eyes and ears** of an organisation‚Äôs defence.
