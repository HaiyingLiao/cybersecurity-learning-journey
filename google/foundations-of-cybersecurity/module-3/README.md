# What I Learned

### Security frameworks guide organisations in managing risk

Frameworks provide structure for:

- Identifying security goals
- Setting guidelines
- Implementing processes
- Monitoring & communicating results

Frameworks help protect PII/SPII, meet compliance requirements, and align security with business goals.

### Controls reduce specific risks

Security controls are safeguards (policies, processes, tools) used to prevent, detect, or mitigate threats.  
Example: requiring mandatory privacy training to reduce human-error risk.

### The CIA triad underpins all controls

- **Confidentiality:** Only authorised users can access data.
- **Integrity:** Data must remain accurate and unaltered.
- **Availability:** Data must be accessible when needed.

CIA helps organisations evaluate asset risk and choose appropriate controls.

# Security Frameworks & Compliance

### Security Lifecycle

A continuous process of managing risk, creating standards, enforcing policies, and maintaining compliance.

### Core components of frameworks

1. **Identify & document goals** (e.g., GDPR compliance).
2. **Set guidelines** (e.g., how to handle user data requests).
3. **Implement processes** (e.g., verify user identity for profile changes).
4. **Monitor & report** (e.g., detecting a GDPR-related incident).

# Major Frameworks & Standards

## NIST Frameworks

- **NIST CSF (Cybersecurity Framework):** Guides risk management and incident response.
- **NIST RMF (Risk Management Framework):** Addresses the lifecycle of assessing and authorising systems.

## FERC-NERC (Power Grid Compliance)

For organisations tied to the North American power grid â€” must follow Critical Infrastructure Protection (CIP) standards.

## FedRAMP

Standardises security for U.S. government cloud services.

## CIS Controls

Actionable security controls to defend against known cyber threats.

## GDPR

EU regulation protecting personal data and privacy.  
Key points:

- Must disclose breaches within 72 hours
- Must be transparent about data use
- Heavy fines for non-compliance

## PCI DSS

Global standard for secure handling of credit card data.

## HIPAA / HITRUST

Protects patient health information (PHI).  
Requires privacy, security, and breach notification processes.

## ISO standards

International standards across technology, management, and processes.

## SOC 1 / SOC 2

Reports assessing organisational security, integrity, privacy, and risk management practices.

# Threat Actors & Their Motivations

- **APTs (Advanced Persistent Threats):** Highly skilled groups targeting governments & corporations.
- **Insider threats:** Employees/contractors misusing access.
- **Hacktivists:** Motivated by political/social causes.
- **Hackers:**
  - **Authorised (ethical)**
  - **Semi-authorised (researchers)**
  - **Unauthorised (malicious)**

Understanding attacker motivations helps organisations prioritise risks.

# Ethics in Cybersecurity

Security professionals must:

- Protect confidentiality and privacy
- Follow laws and regulations
- Remain unbiased and evidence-based
- Act responsibly and transparently
- Continuously maintain their skills

### Counterattacks

- **Illegal in the U.S.** except for federal/military personnel
- Internationally permitted only under strict conditions
- Generally discouraged because it escalates risk

Ethics guide behaviour when dealing with sensitive data and evolving threats.
